# 3Sum — Research & Foundations

Finding triples that sum to zero. The O(n²) sort-and-two-pointer solution is essentially optimal — 3SUM is a landmark problem in fine-grained complexity.

- **A. Gajentaan & M. H. Overmars (1995), “On a class of O(n²) problems in computational geometry,”** *Computational Geometry: Theory and Applications* 5(3):165–185. DOI: 10.1016/0925-7721(95)00022-2. Introduced **3SUM-hardness** — 3SUM is the base problem conjectured to have no truly sub-quadratic algorithm. https://doi.org/10.1016/0925-7721(95)00022-2

**Why it matters here:** Gajentaan & Overmars (1995) made 3SUM the base of a large class of **3SUM-hard** problems; the widely-held 3SUM conjecture says no truly sub-quadratic algorithm exists, which is why O(n²) here is expected.
