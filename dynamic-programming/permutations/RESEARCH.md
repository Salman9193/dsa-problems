# Permutations — Research & Foundations

Enumerating all orderings — backtracking that fixes one position at a time and undoes choices on return.

- **S. W. Golomb & L. D. Baumert (1965), “Backtrack programming,”** *Journal of the ACM* 12(4):516–524. The paper that formalised **backtracking** — build partial solutions and abandon a branch as soon as it cannot lead to a valid completion.

**Why it matters here:** Golomb–Baumert (1965) formalised this generate-and-prune search; permutations are its textbook instance.
