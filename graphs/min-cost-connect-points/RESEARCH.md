# Min Cost Connect Points — Research & Foundations

Minimum spanning tree on a complete graph whose edge weights are Manhattan distances — the classic MST problem.

- **R. C. Prim (1957), “Shortest connection networks and some generalizations,”** *Bell System Technical Journal* 36(6):1389–1401. Prim’s MST algorithm.
- **J. B. Kruskal (1956), “On the shortest spanning subtree of a graph and the traveling salesman problem,”** *Proceedings of the American Mathematical Society* 7(1):48–50. Kruskal’s MST algorithm.

**Why it matters here:** Connecting all points at minimum total cost is exactly an MST; Prim’s algorithm suits the dense complete graph, Kruskal’s the edge-sorted view.
